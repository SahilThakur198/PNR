How to Check PNR Status

Learn the step-by-step process to check your PNR status online, via SMS, or through railway enquiry counters. Understand what different status codes like CNF, RAC, WL mean for your journey.

Checking your PNR status is crucial for a hassle-free train journey. Here's how:

  • Online: The most common method. Visit the official Indian Railways (IRCTC) website or use reliable third-party websites and apps like Rail Sarthi. Simply enter your 10-digit PNR number to get your current PNR status online.
  • SMS: Send an SMS with your PNR number to 139 (e.g., PNR <Your PNR Number>). This is useful when internet access is limited.
  • Railway Enquiry Counters: Visit any railway station enquiry counter with your PNR number.

Understanding PNR Status Codes:

  • CNF (Confirmed): Your seat is confirmed. Coach and seat numbers are usually available after chart preparation.
  • RAC (Reservation Against Cancellation): You are likely to get a berth, which you might have to share initially. It usually gets confirmed.
  • WL (Waiting List): Your ticket is not yet confirmed. It moves up as other passengers cancel. GNWL (General Waiting List) has the highest confirmation chances, followed by TQWL (Tatkal Waiting List), PQWL (Pooled Quota Waiting List), and RLWL (Remote Location Waiting List).
  • CAN (Cancelled): The ticket has been cancelled.
  • NOSB (No Seat Berth): For child passengers below 5 years for whom no seat is opted.

Always double-check your PNR status before your journey, especially a few hours before departure, as chart preparation finalizes seat allocations. Knowing your IRCTC PNR status helps you plan better.

Understanding PNR Numbers

Discover what your 10-digit PNR (Passenger Name Record) number signifies. Each part of the PNR holds specific information about your train booking, from passenger details to journey class.

Read More →

What is a PNR Number? PNR stands for "Passenger Name Record". It's a unique 10-digit number automatically generated by the Indian Railways' Computerized Reservation System (CRS) when you book a train ticket. This PNR number is your primary reference for all journey-related queries and verifications.

Information Contained in Your PNR:

  • Passenger Details: Name, age, gender, and berth preference (if any) of all passengers on the ticket.
  • Train Details: Train number and name.
  • Journey Details: Date of journey, scheduled departure and arrival times, originating station, and destination station.
  • Class of Travel: Such as Sleeper (SL), 3rd AC (3A), 2nd AC (2A), 1st AC (1A), Chair Car (CC), etc.
  • Booking Status: The status of your ticket at the time of booking (e.g., CNF, WL).
  • Current Status: The real-time status of your ticket (e.g., CNF with seat number, RAC, WL position). This is what you check using a PNR checker.
  • Coach and Seat/Berth Number: Assigned after chart preparation if the ticket is confirmed.
  • Fare Details: Total fare paid for the ticket.

The PNR number is essential for checking your reservation status, for the Ticket Checking Examiner (TTE) on the train to verify your booking, and for any modifications or cancellations. Keep your train ticket PNR information handy and secure. Understanding your PNR details can make your Indian Railways experience smoother.

Essential Train Travel Tips

Make your Indian Railways journey comfortable and safe with these tips:

  • Packing Smart:
    • Travel light but carry essentials: Valid ID proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.), train ticket (digital or print), seasonal clothing.
    • Pack a small first-aid kit, toiletries, a power bank, and a lock and chain for your luggage.
  • Food and Water:
    • You can pre-book meals via IRCTC eCatering, purchase from the pantry car (if available), or buy from authorized vendors.
    • Carry your own water bottle and refill it, or buy sealed bottles. It's wise to pack some non-perishable snacks too.
  • Safety and Security:
    • Secure your luggage under your seat or berth using a chain and lock.
    • Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables safe. Avoid accepting food from strangers.
    • Note down Railway Protection Force (RPF) helpline numbers (e.g., 139, 182).
  • During the Journey:
    • Confirm your coach and seat number before boarding.
    • Keep your ticket and ID handy for TTE checking.
    • Use apps like Rail Sarthi to track your train's live running status and expected arrival times.
    • Maintain hygiene and help keep the train clean.
  • Dealing with Issues:
    • For delays, check the live train status.
    • In case of lost luggage or medical emergencies, contact the TTE, RPF, or station master immediately.

A little preparation can go a long way in ensuring a pleasant train journey in India. This IRCTC travel guide aims to help you travel smart!
